Summary

We analyzed 43,018 neurons across 12 Cre lines in 243 mice (Allen Brain Observatory), computing pairwise representational similarity (linear CKA) across 100+ populations under four stimulus conditions.

Inhibitory populations cluster by cell type, not anatomy.

SST, VIP, and PV form distinct groups for natural scenes but not gratings, regardless of visual area or cortical layer.

Natural scenes and natural movies produce consistent cell-type coding structure.

Layer 5/6 excitatory types (Tlx3, Fezf2, Ntsr1) show high mutual similarity. Nr5a1, also layer 5, does not. Cortical layer alone does not determine population coding.

Shared representational structure is driven by fidelity to sensory input.

Pixel-level analysis shows populations with high mutual similarity also preserve the geometry of the high-dimensional visual input. Layer 5/6 excitatory cells and VIP show highest similarity; Nr5a1 does not.

Cross-animal consistency is stimulus-selective.

Most cell types show higher mouse-to-mouse representational similarity for natural scenes than gratings (all p<0.001). Nr5a1 shows no difference (p=0.50), consistent with its specialization for dynamic features.
